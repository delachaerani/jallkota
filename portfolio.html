Bekasi Typical Foods

Akar Kelapa (Coconut Root Cookies)

Named for their resemblance to the root of a coconut, these cookies are crispy on the outside and chewy on the inside. It's a bonus that these cookies taste both salty and sweet. The white rice flour, coconut, and sago flour in this cake give it a unique texture.

Pletok Beer

Pletok beer, often known as Betawi beer, has been a favorite beverage among the Betawi people since colonial times. Ginger, red ginger, lemongrass, turmeric, secang wood, cinnamon, black pepper, pandan leaves, lime leaves, nutmeg, cardamom, mace, and cloves, together with sugar and salt, go into the decoction that makes up this beverage.

Dongkal Cake

Dodongkal is a classic market snack composed of nearly identical ingredients to Putu cake. Namely, palm sugar, coconut meat, and rice flour. In Dodongkal, palm sugar is alternated with rice flour. Dodongkal cakes are printed in a bamboo-woven steamer. Using a particular "seeng" boiler for steaming. Dodongkal is distinguished by the addition of grated coconut and rice flour for dodongkal that has never been dyed..

Ketapang Cookies

These cookies are created with flour, margarine, sugar, coconut milk, salt, eggs, and vanilla. The ingredients are combined to create a dough. The dough is then divided into pieces that resemble Ketapang seeds and fried till golden yellow. Because of its similarity in shape to Ketapang Beans, these cookies from Betawi are also known as Seed Ketapang cookies.

Rangi cake

Rangi cake, also known as sago rangi, is formed with starch and grated coconut cooked in a particular mold over a small stove and covered to cook quickly. Brown sugar thickened with starch tops rangi cake. Brown sugar is blended with jackfruit, pineapple, or durian for flavor. Rangi Cake comes from Digarar Wangi. Composing means cooking without oil and utilizing wood embers. This cooking method makes Rangi cake tasty. Sago palm flour makes Rangi cakes chewy. The grated coconut will provide oil and thick coconut milk that will bind the Rangi cake dough.

Gabus Pucung

According to historical records, the Betawi people, particularly those who lived in Bekasi during the Dutch colonial period, were unable to afford the high price of carp, tilapia, and milkfish, so they developed a unique method of preserving and preparing the fish known as cork pucung. As a processed food, sayur cork pucung has come to represent the cuisine of the Bekasi area.
