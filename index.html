    Welcome to JALLKOTA

    Jallkota translates to "Jalan-jalan ke Kota" from the Indonesian phrase "Travel to the City". When traveling to a city in a foreign country, we are frequently confused about where to go and how to get there, and if we feel perplexed or need information urgently, it takes time to obtain it.

    JALLKOTA

    Therefore, this program was developed with the intention of facilitating city mobility for its users. For instance, we designed a program for those traveling to Bekasi, West Java, Indonesia. And with the goal that this program's successful implementation can be replicated in other cities.

    Definition Bekasi

    The city of bekasi was one of the cities in the western province of Java, Indonesia. It belonged to the members associated with jabodetachment and was made the fourth largest city in Indonesia.

    History of Bekasi City

    Formerly the capital of the Tarumanagara empire, Bekasi was originally known as Jayagiri. The Tarumanagara Kingdom was one of the largest West Java kingdoms ever to exist. When the monument inscription was discovered in Bekasi, it stated that a river named Chandrabaga once traversed the city. Chandrabaga's name then evolved to Bhagasasi and Bhagassi. The moniker Bhagasi eventually survived and evolved into the precursor to the present-day name of the city of Bekasi. Throughout the conflict, Bekasi was known by many as the "city of Patriots." The 1945 outbreak of a major war in this city has tested the patriotism of the Bekasi populace.
